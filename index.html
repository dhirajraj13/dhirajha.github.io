// About Me

Computational Chemist
& Quantum Researcher

I'm a PhD Researcher at Stony Brook University, focusing on the evolution of electronic coherence in molecular systems using Ehrenfest dynamics. My work bridges rigorous electronic structure methods with modern AI/ML tools to advance sustainable energy science.

With an M.Sc. from IIT Guwahati (CPI 8.88/10), I've worked on excited-state photochemistry, green hydrogen catalysis, and data-driven battery materials — combining theory, computation, and machine learning into a cohesive research philosophy.

Beyond research, I serve as a Graduate Teaching Assistant at SBU, an Executive Board Member of the Indian Graduate Student Association, and a member of the American Chemical Society.

Projects & Investigations

01
Nonadiabatic Dynamics Jan 2026 – Present

Electronic Coherence, Decoherence & Recoherence

Investigates the evolution of electronic coherence in molecular systems using Ehrenfest dynamics with the TAB method to model decoherence and explores recoherence phenomena in nonadiabatic molecular dynamics.

Ehrenfest Dynamics Gaussian Fortran TAB Method
02
Catalysis / Green H₂ Nov 2025 – Jan 2026

Oxygen Evolution Reaction on IrO₂ and Related Oxides

DFT-based catalytic pathway analysis identifying active sites, reaction energetics, and stability trends relevant for improving water-splitting performance — an essential step in green hydrogen production.

DFT VASP Water Splitting
03
Machine Learning Oct – Nov 2025

Li-Ion Battery Free Energy Predictions via ML

Developed ML models to evaluate and rank key physical and chemical descriptors for predicting free energy in Li-ion battery systems, supporting faster and more reliable materials screening.

Machine Learning Python Li-Ion Batteries
04
Spectroscopy Aug – Oct 2025

XPES Signatures in Salicylideneaniline

Computational investigation using CASPT2 and DFT to advance accurate computational spectroscopy and validate methodological consistency in predicting core-level ionization energies for complex organic systems.

CASPT2 DFT XPES
05
Master's Thesis May 2024 – May 2025

Effect of Fluorination on Excited-State Properties in N-Salicylideneaniline

Conducted electronic structure calculations to study how fluorination modulates the excited-state properties of N-Salicylideneaniline. The research aims to improve optoelectronic properties by understanding photophysical and photochemical behaviour under substituent effects. Supervisor: Prof. Aditya N. Panda, IIT Guwahati.

Electronic Structure Gaussian Excited States Optoelectronics
